From firmware to intelligence: An embedded engineer’s AI learning notes

This article records an embedded software engineer’s personal exploration process in the AI wave. From the initial confusion and resistance, to using project-driven methods to break the learning deadlock, to making up for shortcomings in mathematics, and using Feynman techniques to deepen understanding, the author gradually found his own position - the intersection of embedded × AI. Directions such as edge reasoning, industrial predictive maintenance, and TinyML deployment not only require solid underlying firmware skills, but also rely on AI model optimization and implementation capabilities, forming a core competitiveness that is difficult for pure algorithm engineers to replicate. The article does not contain high-pitched remarks, only the real insights of an engineer late at night: In this era of reshaping everything, the most stable moat is the composite ability that others cannot cross.
